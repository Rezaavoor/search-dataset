Key findings: The query adapter (+38.8% nDCG@10) outperforms all off-the-shelf models on the test split, confirming it generalises beyond training data. Voyage-4-large symmetric is the strongest standalone model (+28.1%), beating the baseline by a wide margin. The asymmetric Voyage configurations (voyage-4 / voyage-4-lite as query encoder) remain competitive — voyage-4-lite sacrifices only 0.3 nDCG@10 points vs voyage-4, making it an efficient production trade-off.

Adapter transfer analysis: The adapter provides +18.98pp nDCG@10 on the in-domain legal corpus but is neutral on external benchmarks (-0.21pp on ARCChallenge, +0.25pp on BarExamQA). This confirms the adapter is domain-specific: it improves retrieval on the corpus it was trained for without degrading general performance. Voyage-4-large is the strongest general-purpose model, dominating both external benchmarks (43.8% on ARCChallenge, 66.7% on BarExamQA).
